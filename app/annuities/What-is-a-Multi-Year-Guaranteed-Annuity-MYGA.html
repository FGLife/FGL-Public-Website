What is a Multi Year Guaranteed Annuity (MYGA)?

Need Relief from Low Interest Rates in Retirement?

It’s hard to imagine that the 10 Year Treasury was over 5.4% on January 1, 2001. For someone 65 years old today, that was an important year. Sometime in the next 365 days, they will turn 50, receive the infamous card in the mail, and most likely begin to start thinking about their retirement. That retirement plan could have based all savings assumptions on the 5% interest rate remaining stable into the future.

As we all know, the future did not cooperate with those plans.

Today, we find ourselves in a less than desirable position. We raised our kids. We saved money. We worked many long years for a company and earned a respectable pension. We checked all the boxes that people expected of us. Yet we still find ourselves short of money. Today, the most competitive money market rates hover around 1%. Top yielding CDs offer only around 2%.

What do you do if you want to take the grandchildren on a trip or buy a new car? One option is to dip into the principal of your savings. The other could be to take a loan. Both options either reduce your future income potential or the legacy you planned to leave your children.

Our 5-year Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity (MYGA) could be a good solution for finding a higher interest rate on some of your assets to help better meet your expenses today. A MYGA works much like a CD.

Our MYGA will pay you a competitive interest rate for the next five years.

If you purchase a MYGA, we will pay you interest on your single premium deposit each year for the next five years. You can withdraw the interest each year. Early withdrawal of the initial premium will be subject to penalty that decreases each year.

How Does FG Guarantee-Platinum® Work?

Why don’t people like annuities?

Annuities can seem complicated, but so can planning for retirement. As hard as it is to build a retirement nest egg, it can be even harder to convert those assets into a predictable stream of income. Make certain you understand how any annuity that you may purchase works, the benefits it provides and all restrictions on early withdrawals.

What is a multi-year guaranteed annuity?

A multi-year guaranteed annuity, or MYGA, works a lot like a bank CD. You submit a check with your application or sign authorization to transfer assets from another institution’s account into your MYGA. We will credit interest daily and apply it to the account value from the date of issue for the next five years.

What is the difference between a MYGA and a CD?

An insurance company, in this case Fidelity & Guaranty Life, will issue a MYGA. A bank will issue a CD. The two products function very similarly. A consumer will purchase either a CD or a MYGA with a single premium or deposit. The institutions will credit the product with a specific amount of interest during the guaranteed period. After the guaranteed period, the consumer can withdraw the initial premium and all credited interest. There are some important differences. Our MYGA pays compound interest on the principal, while many CDs do not. The account value in a MYGA grows tax-free until withdrawal. Unless a CD is held within a tax qualified account like an IRA, interest received from a CD is a taxable event each year.

The FDIC insures CDs. Is your MYGA insured?

The FDIC does insure CDs. Fidelity & Guaranty Life Insurance Company is domiciled in the state of Iowa and subject to regular reviews. The Iowa Insurance Guaranty Association provides limited protections up to $500,000 for all policyholders.

Who should buy a MYGA?

Generally, retirees over the age of 60 find the benefits of the product most attractive. Assets in a MYGA won’t lose money because of equity market downturns. They return a predictable stream of income. However, the premium can generally not be withdrawn prior to the end of the five-year guarantee period without incurring a surrender charge. Any consumer should carefully consider his or her financial situation and potential need for liquidity in the coming years.

What is the least amount of money I can put in a MYGA?

We require at least a $20,000 premium or transfer of assets to purchase a MYGA.

Should I put all of my money in a MYGA?

No. A MYGA generally provides a higher rate of interest than comparable products. However, any premium placed in a MYGA will be subject to penalty charges that decline over time. Carefully consider your liquidity needs before purchasing a MYGA.

What penalty will I pay if I withdraw my entire policy early?

You will not pay a surrender charge if you withdraw up to the amount of accumulated interest. Withdrawals in excess of the accumulated interest on the annuity will be subject to surrender charges. The surrender charges for FG Guarantee-Platinum will be 9% in the first year and decrease by 1% per year throughout the rate guarantee period.

A Market Value Adjustment (MVA) will increase or decrease your surrender value when you make a withdrawal to which a surrender charge is applied. The MVA is based on a formula that takes into account changes in yields on U.S. Treasuries between the start of the current guaranteed period and the date of the withdrawal. Generally, if interest rates have risen since you purchased your annuity (or since the start of your current interest rate guarantee period, if later), the MVA will decrease your surrender value. If interest rates have fallen, the MVA will increase your surrender value.

Are there age restrictions to buy a MYGA?

Yes, we will issue MYGAs to individuals through age 0-85.

Can I take early withdrawals if I get sick?

In certain states, we offer two riders that allow you to withdraw funds without penalty:

  • Nursing Home Benefit Rider: If you are confined to a licensed nursing home for more than 60 days, and your confinement begins at least one year after the annuity’s date of issue, surrender charges will be waived on withdrawals made during the period of your confinement.
  • Terminal Illness Benefit Rider: If a licensed physician certifies that you have been diagnosed with an illness or condition that causes your life expectancy to be less than one year, and the diagnosis takes place at least one year after the annuity’s date of issue, surrender charges will be waived during this period of terminal illness.

What happens at the end of the 5-year guaranteed rate period?

At the end of the five-year guarantee period, you have the flexibility to continue for another five-year period, based on the original guarantee period, or withdrawal all or some of your money with no surrender charges. At the end of each guarantee period, we will declare a new interest rate for the new guarantee period. If we receive no direction from you, Fidelity & Guaranty Life will automatically renew your annuity for the same period at the then-current interest rate. If you would prefer to withdraw some or all of your account value at the end of any guarantee period, you will have a 30-day window to do so, free of any surrender charges. If you renew after you attain age 91, your surrender charges will be 0% for the duration of your contract.

What happens at the end of the 5-year period if I don’t withdraw my money?

At the end of the fifth year, your annuity will automatically renew into a new five-year interest guarantee period at the then current interest rate. Surrender charges will also be reset, so that in the 6th year of your annuity (or the first year of the renewal period) the surrender charge will again be 9%. Surrender charges will continue to follow the same pattern throughout each renewal period. Maria’s surrender charge will be 8% in the 7th year, 7% in the 8th year, 6% in the 9th year, 5% in the 10th year, 9% in the 11th year (first year of 2nd renewal period), 8% in the 12th year, etc.

Can I change my mind after I have purchased a MYGA?

You have a set number of days (at least 10) to look at the annuity after you buy it. If you decide during that time that you don’t want it, you can return the annuity and get all of your premium back, less any prior withdrawals. Read the cover page of your annuity contract as soon as you receive it to understand how many days you have to decide if you want to keep it.

Who gets the money if I die?

If you should die before the annuity date, your beneficiary will receive the account value of your annuity. Surrender charges do not apply at death. Any gain in the annuity would be subject to income tax. If you should die after the annuity date, any benefits payable to your beneficiary would depend on the income option chosen.

How does this affect my taxes?

The annuity is tax-deferred, which means you don’t pay taxes on the interest it earns until the money is paid to you. When you take payouts or make a withdrawal, you pay ordinary income taxes on the earned interest. Withdrawals are treated as coming from earnings first and then as a return of your premium. Payments under an annuity payment plan are treated as coming partially from earnings and partially as return of premium. You may pay a federal income tax penalty on earnings you withdraw before age 59 ½. If your state imposes a premium tax, it will be deducted from the money you receive.

Buying an annuity within an IRA, 401(k) or other tax- deferred retirement plan doesn’t give you any extra tax benefit. Choose the annuity based on its other features and benefits as well as its risks and costs, not its tax benefits. Please consult your tax advisor regarding your unique situation.

Internal Revenue Code provides that if a non-natural person holds the annuity and such person is not holding as an agent for a natural person, the contract shall not be treated as an annuity contract for income tax purposes.
